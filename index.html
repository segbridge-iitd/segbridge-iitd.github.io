Continual Adaptation of Vision Foundational Models for Semantic Segmentation in Adverse Weather

Nikhil Kumar Jangamreddy1,2,3 Mahsa Baktashmotlagh2 Chetan Arora3

1 UQ-IIT Delhi Research Academy (UQIDRA)
2 The University of Queensland, Australia
3 Indian Institute of Technology Delhi, India

We address the challenge of semantic segmentation under adverse weather and low-light conditions. Our findings show that even strong vision foundation model (VFM) baselines such as Mask2Former with a DINOv2 backbone (M2FD2), struggle under severe distribution shifts induced by extreme variations in weather and illumination. The key insight driving this work is that modern neural representations are highly sensitive to the strength and spatial distribution of color edges in images. Since each weather condition exhibits distinct boundary characteristics (e.g., sharper edges in snow, blurred edges in fog), VFMs face difficulty generalizing across such diverse domains. To overcome this, we propose Continual Test-Time Domain Adaptation (CTDA) of VFMs through a novel Boundary-Guided Query Discovery (BGQD) strategy. Our approach introduces a domain-specific prompt bank that captures and preserves the unique boundary features of each domain. During test time, BGQD leverages high-confidence edges from the input image and integrates domain-specific knowledge to dynamically transform pre-trained object queries. Experiments on four adverse-weather datasets spanning three countries and six weather conditions demonstrate the superiority of our approach. Integrating BGQD with M2FD2 yields substantial gains of 32.3%, 20.3%, 27.3%, and 23.5% over the best existing CTDA method, and 10.2%, 12.1%, 10.4%, and 14.2% over the M2FD2 baseline.
